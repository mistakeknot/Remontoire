# Vision

A self-improving software portfolio that can discover what it should build,
test assumptions cheaply, and compound outcomes into increasingly trustworthy
autonomy, while humans retain authority over policy and production.

## Destination

At maturity, Remontoire continuously assembles evidence from the portfolio,
identifies the decision whose uncertainty has the highest expected cost,
designs the cheapest decisive experiment, delegates it to the best available
harness, and updates its future judgment from the reviewed result.

Autonomy expands only when replayable receipts show that a bounded class of
decisions is reliable. It contracts when evidence weakens. The principal can
always inspect, override, pause, or revoke the loop.
