# Philosophy

## Judgment Over Throughput

The scarce resource is not implementation capacity. It is correct attention.
Remontoire optimizes for uncertainty retired per unit of cost, not tasks closed.

## Experiments Before Commitments

When an important claim is uncertain, prefer a bounded experiment with a
falsifiable hypothesis over a production-sized implementation. The evidence
contract must exist before execution.

## One Bet Per Cycle

A cycle may select zero or one experiment. This cap makes causality legible,
limits blast radius, and forces genuine prioritization.

## Corrective Work Competes

Defects, integration gaps, maintenance, and missing verification enter the same
ranking as new capabilities. Novelty is not leverage by itself.

## Receipts Must Change Behavior

A receipt that is merely stored is a log. A useful receipt updates future
scoring, closes or promotes work with evidence, and changes the next roadmap.

## Earned and Revocable Authority

Autonomy is scoped by action class and supported by observed outcomes. It is
never inferred from a global confidence label. Execution approval and
production landing remain distinct authority gates.

## Mechanism and Policy Stay Separate

Intercore provides durable mechanisms. Ockham supplies policy. Remontoire makes
portfolio judgments within those constraints. None may silently absorb another
component's authority.

## No Parallel Truth

Local artifacts may cache or render canonical state, but Remontoire does not
create a second backlog or operational database. Beads and Intercore remain the
sources of truth.

## Execution Is Delegated

Remontoire selects, constrains, and reviews work. Existing harnesses execute it.
The v0.1 process never grants its executor permission to push, merge, deploy, or
modify files outside the approved experiment boundary.

## Humans Remain Above the Loop

The principal controls intent, approval, and production. Remontoire makes the
decision surface sharper; it does not hide consequential choices behind
automation.
